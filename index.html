Designed to work alongside a Notes field
These picklist values assume a separate Closed Lost Reason Notes free-text field exists on the deal record. The single-select keeps reporting clean and consistent. The notes field is where reps provide context, competitive details, specific objections, or anything that doesn't fit a category neatly. Together they solve the problem this dataset exposed: "Bad Fit: See Notes for Details" tells you nothing on its own.

Design Principles

The current LASSO picklist has 85+ multi-select values leading to inconsistent tagging, "See Notes" as a catch-all, and zero actionable signal on ~15% of deals. This redesign collapses everything into 13 clean single-select values across 4 categories ‚Äî enough specificity to act on, not so many that reps game it.

‚ú¶
Single-select only. Forces a primary reason per deal. Multi-select diffuses accountability and makes reporting meaningless.
‚ú¶
No "See Notes" option. Notes belong in the notes field. Every picklist value should be reportable on its own.
‚ú¶
Mutually exclusive categories. Each value has one home. If a deal is both Bad Fit and Timing, reps pick the primary reason.
‚ú¶
Actionable segmentation. Every value maps to a distinct follow-up action ‚Äî nurture, disqualify, escalate, or re-price.
‚ú¶
Honest competitive tracking. Competitive losses need their own value so displacement doesn't hide in Bad Fit.
